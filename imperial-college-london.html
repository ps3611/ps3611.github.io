Imperial is a science-based institution, consistently ranked amongst the world's top universities.

Statistics  MSc

This program provides outstanding training in both theoretical and applied statistics. In the autumn term, students obtain advanced knowledge of probability theory and fundamentals of statistical inference. They are also taught numerous skills to tackle the challenges of modern applied and computational statistics.
In the spring term, the wide range of optional courses offered allows students to deepen their knowledge in the statistical fields of their interest. I chose to take courses in Machine Learning, Financial Econometrics, Retail Finance, Survival Models with Actuarial Applications, Bayesian Inference and Medical Statistics.

In the summer term, students take up a research project of their own. This allowed me to fulfill my lifelong dream of combining my beloved sport of tennis with the world of statistical analysis. Building on projects from my internship at ATASS Sports as well as on tools acquired during this Masters program, I wrote my thesis in three months. It was graded First Class. Mathematics with Statistics for Finance  BSc

This program provides first-class teaching in the three main areas of mathematics: statistics, pure and applied mathematics. During the first two years of the course, students are given a firm foundation in these three building blocks as well a introduction to numerical mathematics and programming.
lIn the third and final year, students specialise in the mathematical area of their interest. Over the years, my curiosity clearly leaned towards numerical and data-driven problems, so I decided to go down the Statistics path, taking courses like Statistical Modelling, Stochastic Simulations, Markov Chains and Credit Scoring.

I also chose to undertake a one-month research project in which I developed a statistical model allowing the Statistics Department to predict the offer-acceptance-probability of prospective students.
