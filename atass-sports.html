ATASS Sports is a statistical research consultancy providing quality modelling and analysis for the sports industry.

Summer Internship

I spent the summer of 2014 working as a research intern for ATASS Sports in Exeter. I was chosen from more than 120 applicants to be part of a team of 9 interns (I was the only non-Oxford/Cambridge intern!).

During the course of the year, the company's research team gathered a list of projects that they themselves did not have time to look into, and in the summer they gave it to us interns to do. We where put into groups of three to work on a specific project for two weeks. At the end of the two weeks, we presented our findings back to the research team and to members of the company's management. Here is the list of projects that I was involve in:
​
  • ​Project 1:   Use the Elo rating system to predict the match-win probability of players in a tennis match.
  • ​Project 2:   How good is it to assume that sets in tennis are independent?
  • ​Project 3:   Is serving first in a tennis set a statistically significant advantage?
  • ​Project 4:   Find a way to accurately predict the batting order of a cricket team for any given match.
  • ​Project 5: Build a model that tracks the evolution of the match-win probability of a football team         during a live match.   

This internship was an amazing opportunity for me to dive deep into the world of statistical research in my beloved field of sports. What is more, I ended up using Projects 1 and 5 as heavy inspiration for my Masters thesis! 

​Computing skills developed: R (advanced), Shiny (intermediate), Powerpoint (advanced)
